Crimson Saga

New Biome

Crimson Eden

Once upon a time, a civilization believing in decency and one God flourished in these crimson sands. The crosses in this biome contained the accumulated blood of innocent and brutally executed inhabitants.

Thus, Crimson Eden turned into an almost dead desert, where only some architectures of past generations remained, and crimson crosses, which were, surprisingly, extremely durable. The entire desert was soaked in anger and injustice.

All these accumulated feelings, held for a long time in the soil, gave the opportunity for one of the most ancient Gods of vengeance to be born. Alas, soon after this, many left this place. Now this is clearly not Eden, but only a desert soaked in negative thoughts and condemnations.

Boss

Tenderot

Tenderot is the most current God of vengeance after the death of Charon. Visually, it is a huge creature, remotely resembling a worm, with scales made of the strongest material - nitozium.

This God contradicts his divine brethren with his principles. He sees revenge in everything, and his obsession has long become like "blindfold" - he sees no progress in anyone or anything. His perception of the world is distorted by the eternal thirst for retribution, which makes him both powerful and blind to the true nature of things.

Boss

Death Goddess

An ancient deity personifying not the end, but the transition. Unlike Tenderot, whose essence is permeated with personal resentment and thirst for revenge, the Death Goddess represents an impersonal, inexorable force of natural order.

She does not feel hatred, does not strive for destruction for the sake of destruction. Her existence is a guarantee that everything that has a beginning will have an end. In her presence, time flows differently, and matter reminds of its mortality.

Her appearance in Crimson Eden was a response to the violation of the natural balance caused by the birth of Tenderot from accumulated injustice. She is the necessary opposite, a force striving to restore disturbed harmony, even if it requires putting an end to an entire era.
